Please consider our manuscript, “A positive-electrode model separating iodine saturation from accessibility loss in zinc–iodine flow batteries,” for publication as a Research Paper in *Journal of Power Sources*.

The porous positive electrode of a zinc–iodine flow battery must accommodate dissolved and solid iodine while retaining electrochemically accessible area. Full-cell voltage combines these states and cannot resolve them independently. This study develops a state-resolved, two-dimensional positive-electrode model that separates free-I2 supersaturation, solid-I2 inventory and calibrated accessibility loss. Molecular and mesoscale calculations provide bounded transport and accessibility inputs rather than inferred deposit morphology.

At 40 mA cm−2, the baseline simulation reaches electrode-average saturation at 83.0 mAh cm−2 and 50% calibrated accessibility loss at 99.6 mAh cm−2. In a matched simulation, changing only the inventory-to-accessibility relation shifts the saturation capacity by 0.002 mAh cm−2, prevents 50% accessibility loss within 120 mAh cm−2 and lowers the terminal voltage by 288 mV. The comparison separates an upstream saturation event from downstream accessibility feedback and shows why voltage alone cannot identify the inventory-to-accessibility relation. Applied current and oxidized-iodine diffusivity produce large, directionally interpretable modeled shifts in saturation capacity over the selected simulated ranges.

The manuscript fits the journal’s coverage of redox flow batteries, component engineering and multiscale electrochemical modeling. Existing full-cell records establish the device-scale late-charge problem and its variation across a recorded same-cell current sequence; they are kept distinct from measurements of the modeled internal positive-electrode states. For data integrity, the manuscript also discloses EXP-META-001: legacy acquisition fields labeled “NH4Cl” refer to experiments performed with NH4Br. Raw filenames, contents and hashes are preserved, while derived tables use the corrected identity. NH4Br is treated only as a representative supporting electrolyte.
