**Figure 6 | Applied current and oxidized-carrier diffusivity shift the averaged
saturation point.** **a,** Positive-electrode averaged saturation
point, `Q_s`, as a function of applied current density, `J`, from full continuum
simulations. Filled circles mark trajectories that cross `S_avg = 1`; values are
obtained by linear interpolation between the two adjacent capacity samples
bracketing the crossing. The line joins only the verified full-capacity
simulations at 20 and 40 mA cm^-2. At 80 mA cm^-2, `S_avg` remains below one
through the simulated endpoint (`Q = 40 mAh cm^-2`), so the open triangle and
upward arrow denote a right-censored observation, `Q_s > 40 mAh cm^-2`, rather
than an exact point. The separate 120 mA cm^-2 simulation crosses at
`Q_s = 8.39 mAh cm^-2`. **b,** `Q_s` versus normalized effective
oxidized-carrier diffusivity, `D_eff/D_0`, from seven full continuum simulations.
Segments join adjacent simulated conditions only; no fit or extrapolation is
shown. Shading marks the MD-informed range, `0.8–1.4 D_0`. **c,** Shifts relative
to the reference saturation point, `Q_s,0 = 83.0202 mAh cm^-2`, for the declared
parameter ranges. Arrows run from the first to the second setting printed at
left, and values at right give the total span in percentage points. Solid lines
and circles denote continuum simulations, dashed lines and diamonds the
analytical flow-dependent boundary-layer scenario, and dotted lines and open
triangles the MD-informed diffusivity range mapped through the simulated
response. The current comparison is restricted to 20–40 mA cm^-2. The linked
felt branch changes thickness and porosity together. For the analytical flow
scenario, `delta` scales as `v^-m` with `m = 0.5`, while the remaining baseline
trajectory is held fixed. `Q_s` is a simulation-derived positive-electrode
quantity, not an experimentally demonstrated full-cell ceiling.
