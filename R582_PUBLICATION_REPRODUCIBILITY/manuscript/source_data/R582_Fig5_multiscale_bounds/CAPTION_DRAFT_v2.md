**Figure 5 | Independent bounds on positive-electrode model inputs.** **a,**
Periodic CP2K BSSE-corrected adsorption-energy ordering for dry single-I2
placement, reported relative to basal carbon. **b,** Five-SOC bulk-MD means for
I-, I3- and I2Br- under
q = 0.8 ECC and formal-charge parameterizations in the representative 1 M ZnI2
+ 4 M NH4Br supporting electrolyte; error bars report propagated
within-trajectory stability. Shading denotes the formal-charge low-end carrier
proxy range (0.425-0.664 x 10^-9 m2 s^-1), and the dashed line marks the
continuum baseline D_eff = 0.50 x 10^-9 m2 s^-1. **c,** Geometric remaining-area
ratio A/A0 for sparse (N = 10^11 m^-2) and dense (N = 10^14 m^-2)
single-fibre placement; lines connect computed nodes, including the exact
zero-solid boundary. This geometric comparator is distinct from the native
COMSOL accessibility, A_bare/A0 = R_theta T_pore, where
R_theta = 1 - theta_cal and T_pore = (K/K0)^1/2. **d,** Six-law permeability
envelope for an idealized pore network
over the baseline solid-I2 range; the inset retains the full computed range. At
epsilon_s = 3.219 x 10^-3, K/K0 = 0.981-0.992. These lower-scale calculations
bound molecular mobility, geometric remaining area and permeability
separately. They do not determine the voltage-calibrated R_theta relation or
identify deposit morphology or pore closure.

\caption{\textbf{Independent bounds on positive-electrode model inputs.}
(a)~Periodic CP2K BSSE-corrected adsorption-energy ordering for dry
single-\ce{I2} placement, reported relative to basal carbon. (b)~Five-SOC
bulk-MD means for \ce{I^-}, \ce{I3^-}
and \ce{I2Br^-} under $q=0.8$ ECC and formal-charge parameterizations in the
representative \SI{1}{M}~\ce{ZnI2}$+$\SI{4}{M}~\ce{NH4Br} supporting
electrolyte; error bars report propagated within-trajectory stability. Shading
denotes the formal-charge low-end carrier-proxy range
($0.425$--$0.664\times10^{-9}~\si{m^2.s^{-1}}$), and the dashed line marks the
continuum baseline $D_{\mathrm{eff}}=0.50\times10^{-9}~\si{m^2.s^{-1}}$.
(c)~Geometric remaining-area ratio $A/A_0$ for sparse
($N=10^{11}~\si{m^{-2}}$) and dense ($N=10^{14}~\si{m^{-2}}$) single-fibre
placement; lines connect computed nodes, including the exact zero-solid
boundary. This geometric comparator is distinct from the native COMSOL
accessibility, $A_{\mathrm{bare}}/A_0=R_{\theta}T_{\mathrm{pore}}$, where
$R_{\theta}=1-\theta_{\mathrm{cal}}$ and
$T_{\mathrm{pore}}=(K/K_0)^{1/2}$. (d)~Six-law permeability envelope for an
idealized pore network over the baseline solid-\ce{I2} range; the inset retains
the full computed range. At $\varepsilon_s=3.219\times10^{-3}$,
$K/K_0=0.981$--$0.992$. These lower-scale calculations bound molecular
mobility, geometric remaining area and permeability separately. They do not
determine the voltage-calibrated $R_{\theta}$ relation or identify deposit
morphology or pore closure.}
