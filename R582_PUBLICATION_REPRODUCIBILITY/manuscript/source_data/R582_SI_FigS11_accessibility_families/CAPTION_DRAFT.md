**Figure S11 | Geometric remaining-area families across the tested placement-density range.** **a,** Single-fibre geometric remaining-area fraction, `A/A0`, versus retained-solid fraction, `epsilon_s`, for all four registered comparator families. Each line connects the five computed nodes for the stated precipitation fraction `phi_ppt` and placement density `n_n`; no interpolation or fitted family curve is introduced. The dashed grey trace is the separately calibrated comparator `R_theta = 1 - theta`, calculated as `1 - comsol_theta_ref` at the union of registered abscissae. It is not a geometric `A/A0` family and must not be read as native COMSOL `A_bare/A0`. In the native continuum closure, `A_bare/A0 = R_theta * T_pore`; because `T_pore` is not contained in this S11 node set, that product is not plotted. **b,** Five exact retained-solid nodes shared by the two `phi_ppt = 0.005` geometric families. Raising the prescribed placement density from `10^11` to `10^13 m^-2` changes geometric `A/A0` by 0.085–0.309 over `epsilon_s = 3.39 × 10^-4` to `3.39 × 10^-3`; the largest exact paired difference is 0.309 at the final shared node. These deterministic `E-COMP` families bound the consequence of prescribed single-fibre placement assumptions. They are not measured coverage, reconstructed felt morphology or validation of either the `R_theta` comparator or native COMSOL accessibility.
