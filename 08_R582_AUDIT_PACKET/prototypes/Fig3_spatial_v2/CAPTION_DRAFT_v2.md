**Fig. 3 | Retained solid and calibrated accessibility reshape reaction within the positive electrode.** Continuum-model snapshots are shown at $Q=80$, 100 and 120 mAh cm$^{-2}$, before the mean-saturation coordinate, near the calibrated half-accessibility coordinate, and at late charge, respectively. The capacity rail distinguishes the exported snapshots from $Q_s=83.0202$ and $Q_{f,\mathrm{cal}}=99.5901$ mAh cm$^{-2}$; thus, $Q=100$ mAh cm$^{-2}$ is approximately, not exactly, $Q_{f,\mathrm{cal}}$. The top row shows retained-solid fraction, $\epsilon_s$, on one shared symmetric-logarithmic percent scale. The middle row shows the pointwise remaining calibrated bare-area fraction, $A_\mathrm{bare}/A_0=1-\theta_\mathrm{cal}$, on one shared linear scale. The bottom row shows total reaction-current density, $j_\mathrm{total}$, on one shared logarithmic scale. Six non-positive-current nodes at $Q=100$ are retained as a neutral mask rather than clipped or converted to magnitudes. The maps use the same 3--5 mm through-plane and 0--20 mm flow domains in every column. They are modeled fields, not micrographs or evidence of a unique deposit morphology or pore-blocking front.
