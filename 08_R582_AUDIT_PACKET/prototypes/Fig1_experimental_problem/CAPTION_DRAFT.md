**Fig. 1 | Full-cell records motivate a positive-electrode state analysis.** **a,** The measured cell response contains both electrodes, whereas the model and interpretation focus on the porous positive carbon felt. **b,** Representative cycle-20 charge at 40 mA cm$^{-2}$. The thin grey trace is the registered interpolated voltage, the dark trace is its frozen cubic Savitzky--Golay smooth, and the lower trace is $\mathrm{d}V/\mathrm{d}Q$. The shaded interval contains the voltage-rise feature. $Q_s=83.0$ and $Q_{f,\mathrm{cal}}=99.6$ mAh cm$^{-2}$ are baseline-model coordinates and were not fitted to this voltage trace. **c,** Coulombic efficiency for the same-cell pristine 80--360 mA cm$^{-2}$ rate ladder; vertical bars span the available sequential cycles. The separate 40 mA cm$^{-2}$ pristine cell and the 400 mA cm$^{-2}$ P350C-positive/pristine-negative proxy are deliberately unconnected. **d,** Primary-window rise onset and maximum for cycles 20--23 from one physical cell. Pale spans give extrema across three prespecified derivative windows and are estimator-sensitivity ranges, not confidence intervals. These records motivate, but do not independently validate, the positive-electrode state model.
